# World Development Indicators, 2022 (country-level official statistics)

A cross-section of four official development indicators for 207
countries and territories in 2022, retrieved from the World Bank's World
Development Indicators (WDI) API. The dataset is used by the package
vignettes as a realistic official-statistics example for mixture
clustering (countries group into latent development regimes) and
mixture-of-regressions (the Preston-curve relationship between national
income and life expectancy).

## Usage

``` r
wdi2022
```

## Format

A data frame with 207 rows and 9 columns:

- country:

  Country or territory name (World Bank).

- iso3:

  ISO 3166-1 alpha-3 code.

- region:

  World Bank geographic region.

- income:

  World Bank income classification.

- gdp_pc:

  GDP per capita, constant 2015 US\$ (indicator `NY.GDP.PCAP.KD`).

- life_exp:

  Life expectancy at birth, total, years (`SP.DYN.LE00.IN`).

- fertility:

  Total fertility rate, births per woman (`SP.DYN.TFRT.IN`).

- urban:

  Urban population, percent of total (`SP.URB.TOTL.IN.ZS`).

- year:

  Observation year (2022 for all rows).

## Source

World Bank, World Development Indicators.
`https://databank.worldbank.org/source/world-development-indicators`

## Details

Retrieved 2026-07-02 from `https://api.worldbank.org/v2/` (year 2022;
aggregates such as regions and income groups excluded via the country
metadata endpoint; only countries with complete values on all four
indicators retained). One record (Central African Republic) was excluded
because its retrieved 2022 life-expectancy value (18.8 years) is an
evident source-data artifact.

World Bank data are published under the Creative Commons Attribution 4.0
license (CC BY 4.0); see
`https://datacatalog.worldbank.org/public-licenses`.

## Examples

``` r
data(wdi2022)
summary(log(wdi2022$gdp_pc))
#>    Min. 1st Qu.  Median    Mean 3rd Qu.    Max. 
#>   5.574   7.766   8.806   8.893   9.984  12.275 
```
